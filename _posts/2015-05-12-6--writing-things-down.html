Every time you have a cool idea, a profound thought, or see something you want to remember, write it down! It may seem daunting at first, but once you make it a priority to right down anything and everything that may be of use to you, you will soon reap the benefits, of which there are plenty.

I love my grandparents, but that doesn't mean I can't get distracted by one of the 10,000 other things I think about on a day-to-day basis. I'm 22 years old. I'm in classes, I work, I've made a commitment to pretend I know anything about anything on this blog, and on occasion I waste anywhere from 5 to 35 minutes imagining what I would do if I went back in time, to when I was 7 years old, knowing everything I know now. Just because something is important to me, doesn't mean it won't slip my mind.


There’s a ridiculous amount of hard science, proving that writing any given thing down increases your chances of remembering it, even if you never look at it again. Instead of scraping the internet for some second-hand science, I'll try to describe the benefits of writing things down, from my own experience.


Here is a list of ways you can benefit, by being proactive in writing things down:


#1 - You're able to "prioritize your priorities".
If you make a point of writing down all of the things you need/want to get done, you can actually sort them in order of importance. By tackling my goals individually, and in order of urgency/importance, I find I'm able to work a lot more efficiently. You can also reduce the weight of all your to-do's, by eliminating the ones you simply don't have time for. Now that you've eliminated them, these thoughts no longer linger, clouding your mind and wasting your limited brain power.

#2 - You can constantly refine your processes. And it's fun!!!.
Have you ever found and read some of your old schoolwork? It's interesting to see how you've grown over the years. Perhaps even more interesting, is seeing how much your thoughts and mindset can change in a much smaller timeframe. Everytime you look back on your notes, you're in at least a slightly different mindset than when you initially wrote them down. Each time you look back, you offer a different perspective on your own ideas. That's how the refining process works! And it's exciting to feel yourself learning and growing!

#3 - You can enhance the experience you get from reading a book.
Even if you're just reading for leisure, it can't hurt to learn something! Try using a blank, stiff piece of paper as a bookmark, and writing down each word you cannot provide a clear definition for. You can either look the words up as you go, or take some time every few days to look them all up at once. Although I prefer the feel of a real book, I often use my tablet for harder reads, as I can easily retrieve a word's definition and highlight sections of the book that I might like to review.

#4 - It can really help you get to know yourself.
As cliché as it sounds, I've discovered that this is an excellent way to better understand myself, my thoughts and my priorities. Taking the time to sit down alone and reflect on your own thoughts and ideas in a systematic fashion can seem like a strange concept at first, but it is in itself, a process that you continue to refine in order to understand both yourself and your environment on a more intimate level.


Set goals, write them down, and list them in order of importance. 5 Months ago I had a list of goals, and at the top of that list was "learn to code to make a blog". And here we are.
