Hike back to craig's camp (with video)

Jun 26, 2015


Wednesday night, Craig, Ho-Jin, and I hiked back to Craig's dad's camp, near Hubbards. It's way back in the woods and really isolated. The camp is solar powered - so cool - and you pump your own water, right from the lake. We had some drinks, built a fire, and had a blast. Check out the video from our trip!



The camp was originally built by a Norwegian man, back in 1947... Imagine if someone had told him then that, in 2015, a young man from Korea would be staying there with his friends and telling stories from his time in the Korean army. Gotta love globalization!


Here is our video from our hike, stay, and canoe trip back




At one point, Ho-Jin and I were sitting by the water and he said to me, "This is another thing I learned while being in Canada. Canadian's know how to enjoy life. This is even more important than me learning English."
That meant a lot to me to hear that. Not that I think all Canadian's are wisdom-filled life coaches, with perfect work/life balances, but it was amazing to witness, in real time, my friend learning something about himself and the world he lives in.


It's been a challenge to film, edit, post, and blog about videos constantly, but I'm definitely getting a lot better at the filming and editing part. I'm also getting a better appreciation of what goes into filmmaking. Now, when I watch movies I'm finding myself paying a lot closer attention to the types of shots and lighting used. Just like anything else, having a better understanding of it amplifies the enjoyment I get from it.


I've got to say, in regards to making these videos, it feels so good to just be creating something. I am not a filmmaker. I have no training or real skills, but there is just something so fulfilling about climbing a tree or getting down on my hands and knees to try and get the shot I want. Looking forward to getting better at this, and to sharing it with you guys.


I listened to a podcast recently regarding creativity, and in the interview a Dr. Jonah Lehrer said that, when asked if they are creative, 95% of the students in a kindergarten class will say yes, while 95% of High School students will say no, when asked the same question. He explained that this is a result of society's pressure on students to conform and to stay within the predetermined boundaries set for them, in order to stay safe. While watching a Casey Neistat vlog (video blog) the other day, I came across this bit by Steve Jobs, which might just inspire you to change your answer to "yes", next time someone asks if you are creative or not.
